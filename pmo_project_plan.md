# PMO Project Plan

## Overview

This document outlines the phased plan to establish a Project Management Office (PMO) operating under a **controlling** model. The PMO is designed to deliver governance, improve delivery reliability, provide portfolio visibility, and optimize resource utilization across the organization.

---

## Phase 1 — Define PMO Mandate and Scope

**Objective:** Align leadership on the purpose, boundaries, and operating model of the PMO before any implementation begins.

**Key Activities:**
- Align leadership on why the PMO is being created: governance, delivery reliability, portfolio visibility, and resource optimization.
- Confirm the PMO operating model as **controlling** (enforces standards and processes, approves decisions at key gates).
- Define and approve success criteria for the PMO.
- Secure executive sponsorship and assign an executive sponsor.

**Outputs:**
- PMO mandate document
- Approved success criteria
- Executive sponsor sign-off

---

## Phase 2 — Assess Current Project Environment

**Objective:** Establish a factual baseline of the current state of project delivery, tooling, and governance.

**Key Activities:**
- Review existing project processes, tools, reporting practices, and governance gaps.
- Identify pain points in delivery, budgeting, prioritization, and resource planning through stakeholder interviews and data review.
- Baseline current performance metrics: on-time delivery rate, budget variance, and benefits realization.

**Outputs:**
- Current-state assessment report
- Pain point register
- Performance baseline metrics

---

## Phase 3 — Design the PMO Framework

**Objective:** Define the structural and procedural foundation the PMO will operate on.

**Key Activities:**
- Define governance structure: steering committees, stage gates, and escalation paths.
- Standardize core project artifacts: business case, project charter, schedule, RAID log, status report, and closure report.
- Define portfolio intake, prioritization, and change control processes.

**Outputs:**
- Governance framework document
- Standardized artifact templates (business case, charter, schedule, RAID log, status report, closure report)
- Portfolio intake and change control process guides

---

## Phase 4 — Define Roles, Organization, and Capabilities

**Objective:** Build the human structure and capability required to operate the PMO effectively.

**Key Activities:**
- Create the PMO org structure and role descriptions for: PMO Lead, Portfolio Analyst, Project Managers, and Coordinators.
- Build a skills matrix and develop a training plan for project and program staff.
- Clarify decision rights and a RACI matrix across business units, IT, Finance, and the PMO.

**Outputs:**
- PMO org chart and role descriptions
- Skills matrix and training plan
- Decision rights and RACI matrix

---

## Phase 5 — Select and Implement Tools and Data Standards

**Objective:** Establish a consistent, reliable technology and data foundation for PMO operations.

**Key Activities:**
- Evaluate and select PPM (Project Portfolio Management) tooling, or optimize existing tools, for planning, tracking, and reporting.
- Define data standards, KPI definitions, and dashboard templates.
- Implement a single source of truth for portfolio status and risk visibility.

**Outputs:**
- PPM tool selection decision and implementation plan
- Data standards and KPI definition document
- Dashboard templates
- Configured single source of truth (portfolio status and risks)

---

## Phase 6 — Pilot the PMO Model

**Objective:** Validate the PMO framework, tooling, and processes at a small scale before organization-wide rollout.

**Key Activities:**
- Select and run a pilot with a small set of high-priority or strategic projects.
- Test governance cadence (meeting rhythms, reporting cycles), reporting quality, and stakeholder engagement.
- Capture lessons learned and refine templates and processes before scaling.

**Outputs:**
- Pilot project list and scope
- Pilot execution report
- Lessons learned log
- Refined templates and process updates

---

## Phase 7 — Roll Out PMO Across the Organization

**Objective:** Scale the PMO model to all active projects and embed it into organizational operations.

**Key Activities:**
- Launch a communication and change management plan to inform and engage all affected stakeholders.
- Onboard all active projects into the PMO governance model.
- Establish regular portfolio reviews and executive reporting cycles.

**Outputs:**
- Communication and change management plan
- Onboarding tracker for active projects
- Portfolio review and executive reporting calendar

---

## Phase 8 — Measure, Optimize, and Sustain

**Objective:** Ensure the PMO continuously delivers value and matures over time.

**Key Activities:**
- Track PMO KPIs: delivery predictability, budget adherence, strategic alignment, and benefits realization.
- Conduct quarterly maturity reviews and drive continuous improvement actions.
- Maintain a PMO capability roadmap to guide ongoing evolution.

**Outputs:**
- PMO KPI dashboard and reporting cadence
- Quarterly maturity review reports
- PMO capability roadmap (rolling)
